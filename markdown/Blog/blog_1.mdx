## 1. Credit Score (CIBIL Score)

**What it is:**  
A **CIBIL score** is a three-digit number ranging from **300 to 900** that represents your creditworthiness and repayment history. It is calculated based on your past financial behavior, including loan repayments, credit card usage, and overall debt management.

**Why it matters:**  
The CIBIL score is considered the **most important factor in personal loan approval**. Banks and financial institutions use this score to evaluate the risk of lending money to a borrower.

A higher credit score indicates responsible financial behavior and increases the chances of quick approval and lower interest rates.

**Ideal Score Range:**

- **750+** → Excellent credit profile with highest approval chances and lowest interest rates  
- **700–749** → Good credit score with strong approval probability  
- **600–699** → Average score where approval may still be possible but at slightly higher interest rates  

**Important Note:**  
Even if your CIBIL score is slightly lower than ideal, loan approval may still be possible depending on your **income stability, employer profile, and existing financial obligations**.

## 2. Income & Repayment Capacity

Banks carefully analyze your **income level and repayment ability** before approving a personal loan. The primary objective is to ensure that you can comfortably repay the monthly EMI without financial stress.

### For Salaried Individuals:

- Minimum monthly income generally ranges between **₹15,000 – ₹25,000**
- Stable salary credit into a bank account
- Employment with a recognized organization

The salary requirement may vary depending on **city category, employer reputation, and lender policies**.

### For Self-Employed Individuals:

Self-employed applicants are required to demonstrate stable business income. Lenders typically evaluate:

- Business turnover and profitability
- Consistency of income
- Financial records and tax filings

Common requirements include:

- Last **2 years’ Income Tax Returns (ITR)**
- Business bank statements
- Financial statements if applicable

**Why this is important:**  
Financial institutions want to ensure that the borrower has **sufficient income remaining after expenses to comfortably repay the loan EMI**.

---

## 3. Employment Stability

Employment stability plays a crucial role in determining a borrower’s reliability and repayment capacity.

### For Salaried Applicants:

Banks generally prefer applicants who have:

- At least **6 months to 1 year of experience in the current job**
- Continuous employment history
- Stable salary structure

Frequent job changes or short employment periods may increase the perceived risk for lenders.

### For Self-Employed Applicants:

Business owners or professionals are evaluated based on:

- Business continuity
- Industry stability
- Profit consistency

Typically, lenders prefer applicants whose businesses have been operational for **at least 2 to 3 years**.

**Why lenders consider this factor:**  
Stable employment or business operations indicate **consistent income flow**, which increases the likelihood of timely EMI repayment.

---

## 4. Existing Debt (Debt-to-Income Ratio)

Another important factor considered by lenders is your **Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)**.

This ratio measures the percentage of your income that is already being used to pay existing loan EMIs.

**The Golden Rule:**  
Your total EMIs (existing loans + new loan EMI) should **not exceed 40–50% of your monthly income**.

**Example:**  
If your monthly income is **₹40,000**, your total EMI obligations should ideally remain below **₹20,000**.

This includes payments for:

- Credit card EMIs
- Personal loans
- Car loans
- Home loans
- Consumer loans

**Tip:**  
If you already have multiple active loans, you can consider options such as:

- Loan consolidation  
- Balance transfer to reduce EMI burden  
- Closing small loans before applying for a new one

## 5. Documentation (KYC & Financial Proofs)

Providing the correct documentation is essential for **quick verification, faster processing, and smooth loan disbursal**.

### KYC Documents (Mandatory)

These documents are required for identity and address verification:

- **Aadhaar Card**
- **PAN Card**

### Income Proof – Salaried Applicants

To verify salary and repayment capability, lenders usually require:

- Last **3 months’ salary slips**
- Last **3–6 months’ bank statements**
- Employee ID or employment proof (if required)

### Income Proof – Self-Employed Applicants

Self-employed individuals may need to provide:

- Last **2 years’ Income Tax Returns (ITR)**
- Profit & Loss statement
- Balance Sheet
- Business bank statements

Having all documents ready helps ensure **faster loan approval and quicker disbursal of funds**.

## 6. Relationship with the Bank

If you already maintain a **Savings Account or Salary Account** with a bank, you may be eligible for **pre-approved personal loan offers**.

Banks often extend these offers to customers with a **strong banking history and regular transactions**.

### Benefits of Pre-Approved Loans

- Minimal or zero documentation
- Instant approval
- Faster processing
- Same-day loan disbursal in many cases

These offers are typically communicated through **SMS, email, internet banking, or mobile banking applications**.
